Project Overview
This project features a comprehensive HR Analytics Dashboard designed to track, visualize, and analyze employee turnover (attrition). By transforming raw HR data into actionable insights, this dashboard helps organizations identify the root causes of employee departures and develop data-driven retention strategies.

Key Features & KPI Metrics
The dashboard provides a high-level summary of the workforce through several Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):
Total Employee Count: 1,470
Attrition Count: 237
Attrition Rate: 16.1% (Note: The 5.9% in the visual appears to be a specific filter; I have listed the core metrics shown).
Average Age: 37 Years
Average Salary: $6.5K
Average Tenure: 7.0 Years

Deep Dive Insights:
Demographic Analysis: The 26-35 age group shows the highest attrition (116 employees), suggesting that mid-career professionals are the most likely to leave.
Education Background: Employees with a background in Life Sciences (38%) and Medical (27%) represent the majority of the attrition.
Salary Correlation: There is a clear link between pay and retention. The "Upto 5k" salary slab accounts for the highest turnover (163 employees), indicating that lower-income brackets are higher-risk zones.
Role-Based Attrition: Roles like Laboratory Technician (62) and Sales Executive (57) experience significantly higher turnover compared to managerial positions.
Gender Trends: The data shows higher attrition among Male (140) compared to Female (79) employees.
